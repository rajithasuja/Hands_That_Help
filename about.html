About Us


Hands that Help is a service-oriented, non-profitable organization initiated by a few doctors from Maulana Azad Medical College, Delhi. It was started recently, in the month of August,2020. We help the poor by collecting clothes from donors and distributing them to those who are in need. We also organize campaingns to collect old clothes and distribute where there is a need. We aim to inspire those who have the opportunity to lend their hands and help the needy.

According to the Census of 2011, India has more than 1.7 million homeless residents, of which 938,384 are located in urban areas. These figures, however, grossly underestimate the real numbers of the homeless. Civil society organizations estimate that at least one per cent of the population of urban India is homeless. Based on this, it can be extrapolated that the population of the urban homeless is at least 3 million. In the capital city of Delhi alone, at any given point, civil society estimates place the number of homeless at around 150,000 - 200,000, of which at least 10,000 are women. India also has the highest number of street children in the world but there is no official data on their numbers or adequate schemes to respond to their special needs and concerns.
